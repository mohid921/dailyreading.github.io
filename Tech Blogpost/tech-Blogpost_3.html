7 hidden features on the iPhone keyboard you may not know existed


You spend hours on your iPhone every day, and a large portion of that time is spent typing. By now you've probably learned the basics, like how you can swipe on the keyboard instead of typing or how the globe key can help you switch between different keyboards. But there are some other hidden or weird features on the iPhone keyboard that you might not have come across yet - here are some you should try.



1. Turn your keyboard into a trackpad

One of the biggest challenges with touchscreen software keyboards is text selection. Apple is trying to make it easier for you with the magnifying loop, but you're still not sure where the cursor is and how much text is selected. You can work around this by turning the entire keyboard into a pointer trackpad. Press and hold the spacebar. Now when you move your finger, the pointer will follow you. Tap anywhere on the keyboard with a second finger and you're in text selection mode. Slide your finger, and as you move, you select the text.



2. Shrink down the keyboard to use it with one hand

If you're using a larger iPhone, one-handed input can be cumbersome. But there's a one-handed stealth mode on the iPhone keyboard: long-press the Globe icon, then use one of three keyboard layout options. It's centered by default, but you can attach it to the left or right edge of the screen, making it easier to use with one hand.

3. A long press and a swipe goes a long way

When typing, you often need to add special characters or numbers. You can, of course, switch to the numeric keypad, but there's a much quicker way to do this: swipe from the "123" button and it will immediately switch to the numeric keypad. Now place your finger on the number or character you want to type and lift your finger. You've just entered a number and you're back to the alphabetic keyboard. Similarly, you can press and hold the keys to uncover hidden symbols. We have a complete guide dedicated to this topic where you can see a detailed list of all the hidden symbols. For example, pressing and holding the period (.) key will display the ellipsis (...) key.


4. Create your own text shortcuts

There are some things you have to type over and over, like your email address, your home address, or maybe your favorite catchphrase. There is a hidden feature called Text Shortcuts that allows you to turn a few letters into full-fledged words, sentences or even paragraphs. So, for example, you could just type "twss" and turn it into "that's what she said". Go to Settings > General > Keyboard > Replace Text and tap the “” button in the upper right corner. Type the phrase, the shortcut, and press the "Done" button. The next time you type the shortcut and press the Space key, it will automatically expand to the pre-selected phrase.



5. Use the hidden formatting bar to undo, redo, copy, and paste

We've talked about three-finger gestures for text editing. Swiping left with three fingers quickly undoes your last move (whether it's typing or deleting). But the gestures are inconsistent and not for everyone. That's why we recommend using the hidden allinone format bar. Just three-finger tap the text box to bring up the floating bar. From here, you can undo, redo, copy, paste, and more.


6. Unlock the hidden Emoticon keyboard

Emojis are everywhere. But instead, you can stand out using emojis, like the good old days of AOL. And you don't need a third-party keyboard for that. Adding a Kana or Romaji keyboard in the settings will do the trick. Go to Settings > General > Keyboards > Keyboards > Add New Keyboard. Select the language "Japanese" and add the keyboard "Kana". Press the "Done" button. Now press and hold the Globe key on the keyboard and switch to the Japanese keyboard. Press the "^ _ ^" key and you will see emojis in the top bar. Press the drop-down button to expand this menu and browse through all the available emojis.


7. Make your iPhone forget auto-correct phrases

The iPhone's keyboard predictor is sometimes too good at picking up phrases you frequently come across and will suggest them to you even if you haven't asked for them - and then there's the problem of iPhone registering misspelled words as autocorrect suggestions. There is only one answer to both of these problems: type in your keyboard dictionary and start over. Go to Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone > Reset > Reset Keyboard Dictionary > Reset Dictionary. Now all your weird autocomplete suggestions will be gone.
