What should you read?
The short answer is: anything you can get your hands on.
At one time, remote areas had to rely on librarians to cross the mountains with books stuffed into bags. But this hardly happens today. Anyone can access the vast libraries contained in mobile phones and tablets.
If you're tight on time, take a few minutes a day to blog about a niche topic. If you're looking for an escape, fantasy or historical fiction can take you out of your own surroundings and into an entirely different world.
If you're after a quick career, read the non-fiction advice of someone who has done it before. Think of it as a form of mentoring that you can choose and set up as it fits your schedule.
One thing to keep in mind: don't just read on one device. Also flip through printed books.
Studies have repeatedly shown that people who read print books perform better on tests of the ability to understand and remember more of what they read than those who read the same digital document.
This can be partly explained by the fact that people tend to read print more slowly than reading digital content. There's nothing wrong with watching an entire TV series, beginning and end, in just one weekend - just as there's nothing wrong with eating a big, scrumptious dessert.
But watching television is probably more of a treat than your main source of intellectual stimulation from time to time. Research shows that long-term television viewing, especially for children, can alter the brain in unhealthy ways.


Takeout
Reading is very, very good for you. Research shows that reading regularly:
•improves brain connectivity
•helps increase your vocabulary and comprehension
•allows you to empathize with others
•helps prepare you for sleep
•reduce stress
•reduce blood pressure and heart rate
•fight depressive symptoms
•prevent cognitive decline like you
•contribute to a longer life
It is especially important for children to read as much as possible good because the effect of reading is cumulative. However, it's never too late to start enjoying the many physical and psychological benefits that await you in the pages of this great book.
